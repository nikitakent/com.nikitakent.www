Books

March 2024

Dark Pools

The focus rests on the “darker” issues regarding high-speed-trading, but I also loved this book as a historical overview of the high-speed-trading system. It's also another anecdote to my belief that innovations seeking to solve one problem often create new problems; like Dr Seuss' cat with pink spots.

Too Big To Fail

Reading about financial crises is one of my favourite past-times, and this book is just the best. I finished it on a 12 hour flight from Auckland to Seoul.

Ethereum by Andreas Antonopoulos

Recommend to anyone interested in ledger technologies. Note that most of the later chapters assume some coding knowledge and a massive RAM if you want to get the most out of it.

Meaning and Argument (Ernest Lepore)

I was born an expert in logic, so I didn't gain anything from this book = True.

Auction Theory: Krishna

Despite the weird notation used in this book, this is the go-to for auction theory.

Microeconomics: MWG

I wish I could recommend more Game Theory books but the truth is I always ended up reverting to this textbook for the extensive coverage on many microeconomics topics and its consistent notation usage, which made for less context-switching productivity tax.

Microeconomic Analysis

Hal Varian is now Chief Economist at Google, but before that he wrote a book on microeconomics in plain english. This book saved my life when I was still trying to get a handle on calculus.

The Organised Mind

Alternative title: “How to live and work with technology and not go insane”.

The Japanese Economy

I was blessed to have Professor Hoshi as a teacher in grad school. With good reason, this book released in the 90s solidified his career as one of Japan's top economic experts.

Code

For my fellow Gen Z who missed out on the 80s and 90s computer-building eras.

Two Sided Matching

I was a theory snob, until I wasn't. Finding a matching with attractive properties such as stability, pareto optimality and strategy-proofness could quite possibly make you a millionaire, or at least a Nobel Prize winner.
